Project Equilibrium

  • Client: University of Toronto Innovation Hub
  • Platform: Mobile
  • Sector: Student Mental Health
  • Project date: Fall 2021
  • Group Member: Shradha Anand, Yongyi Chen, Rebecka Ferraro, Yuxin Xu, Keren Yu, Purui Zhang

Project Description

After a week long research session including both primary and secondary research, me and my team identified that majority of University students suffered from anxiety in school, and one key reason was because of the poor work-life balance. At Project Equilibrium, we're hoping to help, by creating an automatic schedule generator tailored for University of Toronto student. So you can get 8 hours of sleep even during exam season.

Product Journey

Product Journey

Sprint 0: User Research

  • After deciding to choose topic around maintaining positive mental health, me and my team conducted a research including primary and secondary research.
  • Together we interviewing 8 UofT student and received over 40 responses from our survey, from departments and different level of study.
  • My job was to conduct 2 semi-structured interview and record their responses.
  • We find that many of our participants has anxiety from peer pressure, procrastination and difficulty with focusing on tasks. these leads to spending a longtime studying ineffectively, resulting in a poor work-life balance

Sprint 1: User Research Analysis

  • Meet Ursula, the most realistic fake human you will ever meet
  • She is one of those student who struggles to have a healthy work-life balance
  • She often procrastinate during her work period resulting panicking in the last minute
  • My team and I created this persona and empathy map and an as-is scenario to display what she might say, think and do.

    • Sprint 2: Requirement Analysis

  • After figuring out what problems students face ever day, we need to know what students needs. So me and the team sat down and generated 5 needs statements together.
  • Knowing what the student needs, we went through 2 CRAZY brain storming sessions
  • We grouped the ideas, voted on them, and created a prioritization grid to see which one is the most impactful and feasible
  • Not sure why our torture devices and futuristic devices didn't make it to the top-right corner of the grid, these are my favorite.
  • During this sprint, we decided to create a time management game, with some extra function like providing positive notification.

    • Sprint 3: Prototyping

    • After knowing what we going to do, here comes my favorite part, creating the prototype.
    • For our lo-fi prototype, we created a avatar customizing, time management app. The idea was to have users earn rewards by complete their task to buy skins for their avatar. But the idea was criticized for not able to be an main attraction for users, so we erased this feature.
    • We used Balsamiq to create our mid-fi, and my job at this sprint was to create a story board
    • Prototype Link: Click Here

    Sprint 4: Evaluation & Next Step

    • Finally, It's time for user testing. We asked 5 uoft students to help us where they were given 4 tasks to complete.
    • We asked them few questions after the testing sessions for feedbacks on the project.
    • We gathered these feedbacks and wrote a list of features and changes we will make in the future.
    • I conducted 2 of the 5 testing sessions
