Project Treasure Box

    Have you ever wanted to sell an item you no longer use on Facebook Marketplace or ebay, but meeting with a complete stranger for a transaction stress you out? Project Treasure Box is your solution. After contacting your trading partner, simply leave or pick up your item at one of our locker location. It's convenient, it's safe, and it's completely contactless.

Product Journey

Product Journey

Ideation

  • After deciding to choose topic around maintaining positive mental health, me and my team conducted a research including primary and secondary research.
  • Together we interviewing 8 UofT student and received over 40 responses from our survey, from departments and different level of study.
  • My job was to conduct 2 semi-structured interview and record their responses.
  • We find that many of our participants has anxiety from peer pressure, procrastination and difficulty with focusing on tasks. these leads to spending a longtime studying ineffectively, resulting in a poor work-life balance.

Sprint 1: User Research Analysis

  • Meet Ursula, the most realistic fake human you will ever meet
  • She is one of those student who struggles to have a healthy work-life balance
  • She often procrastinate during her work period resulting panicking in the last minute
  • My team and I created the persona, empathy map and an as-is scenario to display what she might say, think and do.
  • My version of Ursula was going to be a good character in Les Miserables, just full of tragic. I'm glad my teammate stopped me other wise her as-is scenario would have been a tragic story

    • Sprint 2: Requirement Analysis

  • After figuring out what problems students face ever day, we need to know what students needs. So me and the team sat down and generated 5 needs statements together.
  • Knowing what the student needs, we went through 2 CRAZY brain storming sessions
  • We grouped the ideas, voted on them, and created a prioritization grid to see which one is the most impactful and feasible
  • Not sure why our torture devices and futuristic devices didn't make it to the top-right corner of the grid, these are my FAVORITE.
  • During this sprint, we decided to create a time management game, with some extra function like providing positive notification.

    • Sprint 3: Prototyping

    • After knowing what we going to do, here comes my favorite part, creating the prototype.
    • For our lo-fi prototype, we created an time management app with avatar customizing game. The idea was to have users earn rewards by complete their task to buy skins for their avatar. but sadly, most of the people we asked to review the prototype did not like the idea of the game. Many people felt like it was childish. So we had to abandon the plan 😢
    • Funny story, so I was doubting the game idea during the sprint. I too didn't find avatar game was appealing. My suggestion was to do something like a complete your task for charity. But my crew convinced me to work on the game. Lesson here is when you truly believe something won't work, do your best to convince others, don't give up your own idea just because the majority wins.
    • We used Balsamiq to create our mid-fi, and my job during this sprint was to create a story board.
    • The App will allow users to have a personalized schedule generated for them by linking to their student account. The app will then generate study sessions based on an estimated number of hour the task will take and ensure user have an healthy schedule.

    Sprint 4: Evaluation & Next Step

    • Finally, It's time for user testing. We asked 5 uoft students to help us where they were given 4 tasks to complete.
    • We asked them few questions after the testing sessions for feedbacks on the project.
    • We gathered these feedbacks and wrote a list of features and changes we will make in the future.
    • I gathered 2 of my friends for the testing session. I wasn't sure if they were being nice to me, but they refuse to give me anything too negative despite I gave them the permission to fully blast us. So another lesson here is avoid having your friends coming to your testing sessions because most likely they're bias.

    Summary

    Overall, I'm very proud of what we accomplished here. Despite there's a few hiccups here and there, and something we still need to figure out like how we are going to be estimating how much time we will allocate for study sessions. My teammates are all talented creative individuals who I'm sure will succeed in this industry. This class has gave me a hands on experience of what UX designer do, and I really had a fun time working with my team. The number 1 thing I wish we could change was, having user testing sessions with our other colleagues in the class, it would have pushed me out of my comfort zone and improve both my interview skills and UX testing skills. As for me personally, I didn't get to design the prototype which was something I wanted to do because it is more on the technical side. But other skills I gained in the class like user research, persona building and ux testing, it will definitely be a part of my journey of becoming an UX designer.

    Keren Yu
